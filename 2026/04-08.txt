andrewrk: I don't know the vagaries of the windows network stack, but I would guess reading between the lines of that report, that what's really happening is something along the lines of:

1775660029
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
1. The reporter's bind()ing server application starts communicating with a UDP peer (sockets on both sides are working/listening for traffic), sending some traffic back and forth...

1775660066
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
2. At some random unexpected point, the peer closes their side.  Because it's UDP, there's no FIN or anything - this condition is silent to us so far.

1775660122
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
3. Then our side tries to do another send.  Because it's UDP (connectionless/unreliable) and there's room in the outbound socket buffer, the OS reports immediate success when the outbound packet lands in a buffer, for it to send off in the background at its leisure

1775660168
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
4. When the OS gets around to sending it, the remote side's port isn't open, and sends back an ICMP Port Unreachable packet to us.  The OS receives this and sets an error flag on our socket that sent the triggering packet.

1775660194
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
5. Then our side tries another receive() call and it returns the above already-pending error that the OS saved in some socket state

1775660913
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
(on Linux, documentation suggests equivalent behavior is possible, returning ECONNREFUSED for this condition)

1775661176
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
(but personally, I've never seen my own UDP listener stuff on Linux run up any error counters related to the above.  maybe it's just luck of the system tunables / firewall / loadbalancer / etc in front of it, that the ICMP-induced errors never reach my recvmsg calls)

1775661861
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
maybe it matters [to why I don't see similar in my DNS stuff on Linux] whether you're being more of a packet-at-a-time kind of UDP server (recvmsg() with address buffer -> sendmsg() with address that was set for you by earlier recvmsg()), whereas in this reported case, it's quite likely they're doing something different, where the sending and receiving calls have source and destination addresses for the 

1775661867
bblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
virtual (application layer) "connection" being talked on.
