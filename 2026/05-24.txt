blblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
tamburlaine: not a direct answer to your question, but the first thing that pops out reading your thread-map stuff is: once you switch to std.Io, technically you're not operating on true threads in the kernel sense.  You're operating now with Tasks, which are abstract units of potentially-concurrent execution.  Task<->OSThread relationships are an implementation detail of the backend std.Io implementation 

1779649582
blblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
(e.g. Threaded, Evented).  The critical thing here is you can't expect "threadlocal" to mean "tasklocal", so you have to move anything that was thread-local into some kind of per-task context/state.

1779649631
blblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
currently, AFAIK, nobody merged work-stealing into std.Io.Threaded, and so it happens to be true that Threaded io.concurrent() Tasks will each be a stable individual OS Thread.

1779649693
blblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
but that's just implementation detail, and I think there's open PRs and/or Issues about implementing work-stealing for Threaded (where e.g. the Task might jump to a different OS Thread after some I/O call has suspended the task for a bit)

1779649708
blblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
and Evented definitely bends the rules, and I think already has work-stealing across OS Threads

1779649723
blblack!~bblack@wikimedia/bblack-WMF
(and that aside, may put several Tasks in one OS Thread)
